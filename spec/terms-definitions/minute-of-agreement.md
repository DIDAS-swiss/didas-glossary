[[def: Minute of Agreement]]

~ A legal procedure available in Scotland: a document drawn up between two or more parties in the presence of their solicitors, without the need for formal court action. In its usual format, it contains numbered paragraphs that record formal but legally binding provisions by which each party has agreed to be bound. If it contains any provision which is enforceable in Scotland, it can be registered in other parts of the United Kingdom for recognition and enforcement, and in EU Member States or EFTA countries. A foreign equivalent may also be known as an Authentic Instrument.
